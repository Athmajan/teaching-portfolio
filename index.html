Teaching Philosophy

The Average Human Attention Span Is Just 8 Seconds

How Can We Expect Students to Stay Focused for Hours?

In today’s fast-paced world, maintaining student attention is harder than ever, especially in long school periods. The constant dopamine hits from screens in the digital era have made it even more challenging for children to focus for extended periods. To keep my classroom energized and focused, I rely on a mix of strategies that make learning active, student-centered, and fun.

Flipped Classrooms

Students review core material at home through short videos or readings, freeing up class time for hands-on problem solving, discussions, and peer interaction.

Gamified Learning

I incorporate game elements like leaderboards, progress tracking, and challenges to boost motivation and make concepts more engaging.

Real-World Projects

By connecting lessons to real-world problems, I help students see the relevance of what they’re learning from coding apps to solving data puzzles.

Think-Pair-Share

A simple but powerful strategy; students think individually, discuss in pairs, then share with the class boosting confidence, participation, and retention.

Interactive ways to enjoy learning CS

In addition to popular platforms like Scratch and Tynker, as well as Code.org , I utilize a variety of interactive tools to help children tap into their creativity and develop problem-solving skills. These include CodinGame and CodeCombat, which offer game-based challenges that make coding both fun and engaging. For older or more advanced students, I introduce platforms like HackerRank, Kaggle, and LeetCode, where they can tackle real-world problems, participate in competitions, and showcase their skills. Together, these tools provide a dynamic learning environment that encourages students to explore, experiment, and build confidence in computer science.
